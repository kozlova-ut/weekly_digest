Will oil drop to $60 or rise to $100 in 2022?
Predicting oil prices is a difficult job at the best of times. Still, it is increasingly difficult as COVID-19 and its variants continue to suspend consumers’ plans and disrupt the balance between oil supply and demand.
We expect crude oil prices to decline in the coming months, as it appears that supply will outpace demand. As a result, the average price of Brent crude will average $70 a barrel during 2022, according to Energy Information Agency (EIA). At the same time, the decline in oil will be an excellent opportunity to buy because the long-term trend will be bullish.
As the global economy recovers, demand for oil fuel will increase, and excess supply will turn into tight supply. This may push prices to $85-$90 a barrel during 2022. In addition, OPEC+ is expected to maintain a tight grip on production, which is an excellent recipe for pushing prices higher.
